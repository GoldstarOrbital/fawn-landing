# Neon Mutualism

Neon Mutualism frames a financial product as a heroic operating system. The visual world is not cute or mascot-led; it is a disciplined collision of interface design, engineering diagrams, kinetic banking panels, and old institutional architecture breaking under pressure. The work should feel meticulously crafted, like a cinematic product launch assembled by someone with master-level control over timing and hierarchy.

Space is structured like a dashboard and staged like an anime title sequence: deep black fields, precise panels, luminous green circuitry, and sudden heroic scale shifts. Text stays minimal and percussive. The design communicates through motion, grids, cards, towers, cracks, and glowing system states rather than explanation.

Color is purposeful: FAWN green as life and agency, cold blue as technical infrastructure, gold as student value, red only as fracture and threat. Every frame should look painstakingly composed, with spacing and component rhythm informed by product design discipline. Buttons, panels, and cards should feel tappable and real, even inside the cinematic sequence.

The emotional reference is arena-scale transformation: small builders versus a massive old banking machine. The motion should be original, legally clean, and music-ready for a heroic track without embedding copyrighted audio. The final artifact should feel like a polished site-native trailer: banking, technology, student power, and the destruction of fee traps.
