We're building the bank we wished existed when we were 19.

FAWN is a fee-free, FDIC-insured checking account built specifically for college students. No monthly fees, no overdraft penalties, no minimum balance - just a real bank account that respects your reality.

Why this exists

Americans paid $12.1 billion in overdraft and NSF fees in 2024 (Financial Health Network) - before counting monthly maintenance, ATM, and other account fees on top. A disproportionate share of that comes from people who can least afford it - students living paycheck-to-paycheck, in their first independent financial environment, often making the kind of small mistakes that turn $4 coffees into $39 catastrophes.

FAWN exists because that's not banking. That's predation dressed up in marble and chrome.

How we make money instead

The same way Chime, Cash App, and Apple Card do: interchange. When you swipe your FAWN debit card, the merchant pays a small fee (usually around 1%) to process the transaction. A portion of that flows back to FAWN. The more you transact, the more we earn. Our incentives line up with yours.

We don't make a cent if you overdraft, can't afford your subscription, or run out of money. That's how it should be.

How it's built

FAWN is built solo by Alex Goldsmith, a Gen Z founder, using Claude Code as a primary engineering partner. The backend is FastAPI on Railway with PostgreSQL. Banking infrastructure runs through Unit - the same regulated BaaS provider that powers Mercury, Brex, and dozens of other vertical neobanks - which means your deposits sit in a real FDIC-insured partner bank, not a prepaid wallet or pooled custodial account.
